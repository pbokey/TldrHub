Finance Courses Should be Required in High School

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Nathan Morse
  • •
  • TLDR

High school students are required to take courses in math, science, English, and social studies, no matter what field they’re going into. Not many people learn about finance, yet everyone has to deal with money in life. After graduating, very few people know anything about saving, budgeting, taxes, or anything related. Every high school student should be required to take a course in personal finance so that we can have a smarter, more money-conscious society.

The Big Puzzle in Economics Today: Why is the Economy Growing so Slowly? - Pulled from Vox

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Nathan Morse
  • •
  • TLDR

As the US economy consistently falls below expectations, conventional economic theory is undermined. In the first half of 2016, we’ve experienced the slowest 6-month growth rate since 2012, contrary to conventional economic theory stating that the bigger the economic downturn, the bigger the boom that follows. Economists can’t come to a consensus, but they examine 8 main possibilities: (1) A lack of innovation, (2) too little spending, (3) company underinvestment due to bad corporate governance, (4) too much debt, (5) Excessive regulation, (6) big city housing regulation, (7) monopoly of economy by big incumbent companies, and (8) a slowly growing and aging population.
