Finance Courses Should be Required in High School

by Nathan Morse • August 5, 2016

too long; didn't read High school students are required to take courses in math, science, English, and social studies, no matter what field they’re going into. Not many people learn about finance, yet everyone has to deal with money in life. After graduating, very few people know anything about saving, budgeting, taxes, or anything related. Every high school student should be required to take a course in personal finance so that we can have a smarter, more money-conscious society.

The Big Puzzle in Economics Today: Why is the Economy Growing so Slowly? - Originally from Vox

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Nathan Morse
  • •
  • TLDR

As the US economy consistently falls below expectations, conventional economic theory is undermined. In the first half of 2016, we’ve experienced the slowest 6-month growth rate since 2012, contrary to conventional economic theory stating that the bigger the economic downturn, the bigger the boom that follows. Economists can’t come to a consensus, but they examine 8 main possibilities: (1) A lack of innovation, (2) too little spending, (3) company underinvestment due to bad corporate governance, (4) too much debt, (5) Excessive regulation, (6) big city housing regulation, (7) monopoly of economy by big incumbent companies, and (8) a slowly growing and aging population.

Cheap Oil Has Killed Nearly 200,000 US Jobs - Originally from CNN

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Matt Egan
  • •
  • TLDR

According to a recent report by outplacement firm Challenger, Gray, and Christmas, cheap oil has fueled an influx of job cuts that might not ease soon. To add to the economic burden, the majority of these jobs are high-paying ones and the oil sector has been cutting jobs as other sectors of the US economy are making moves forward. The industry as a whole, including both diversified oil companies to oil service forms, has made massive layoffs. But some, including Goldman Sachs, believe that such cuts will create an even larger rebound once drilling activity resumes.
