As the US economy consistently falls below expectations, conventional economic theory is undermined. In the first half of 2016, we’ve experienced the slowest 6-month growth rate since 2012, contrary to conventional economic theory stating that the bigger the economic downturn, the bigger the boom that follows. Economists can’t come to a consensus, but they examine 8 main possibilities: (1) A lack of innovation, (2) too little spending, (3) company underinvestment due to bad corporate governance, (4) too much debt, (5) Excessive regulation, (6) big city housing regulation, (7) monopoly of economy by big incumbent companies, and (8) a slowly growing and aging population.
