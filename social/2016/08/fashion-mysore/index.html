Fashion is more than a means of spending money aimlessly. It expresses creativity, showcases culture, and more than anything exudes confidence of both women and men. That being said, it does not mean it comes at the most “affordable” prices.

Oftentimes, people mistake liking luxury brands as a way of showing off wealth, but from my own experience it’s absolutely nothing like that. As a 17-year-old high school student, I own Saint Laurent jewelry, a Burberry coat, a Michael Kors belt and pencil pouch, a Kate Spade handbag and phone case, and lots and lots of luxurious perfume and makeup. Don’t mistake this for bragging; I aspire to be a successful fashion blogger and eventually start my own high street fashion company like Zara and Topshop someday.

I think of fashion as an investment; buy a Chanel bag today for $5,000, and in 2 years, the growth is exponential. For example, Time Magazine’s “Why The Hermès Birkin Bag is a Better Investment Than Gold” by Tara John explains that spending anywhere between $60k to $200k for a Hermès Birkin bag is a more solid investment than spending the same amount in stocks and gold.

As we approach September (fashion’s most impressionable month — think “The September Issue” by Vogue), it is time to add a few timeless pieces to expand your collection.

For a woman:

  • Black Ankle Boots
  • Little Black Dress
  • Signature Lipstick
  • Classic Heels
  • Everyday Bag

For a man:

  • Tailored Suit
  • Watch
  • Leather Jacket
  • White Crew Neck
  • Perfect Sneakers

As you can see, most of these things are probably already in your closet. And if not, it’s best to get shopping.

Sitara Mysore is a high school student in Atlanta, GA.
