Finding Time for Sleep, University Students Weigh In

by Dylan Glicksmn • August 12, 2016 • in Science

too long; didn't read: College comes with an extended set of responsibilities. Students regularly take on internships, jobs, athletics, clubs, and countless other extracurricular activities. This forces them to neglect rest, which is vital not only for their health, but for future academic performance. Signs of sleep deprivation include decreased mood, energy, and physical activity, as well as sleeping during class, procrastination, and depression. Students can increase their quality of sleep by avoiding physical activity, caffeinated beverages, and electronic gadgets near bedtime.
