The mystery of dark matter has remained unexplained since its discovery in 1932. We still cannot account for nearly 96% of the mass of our universe, yet no one’s explanation for this “missing matter” is conclusive. However, a new theory has arrived on the scene, supported by the recent discoveries of LIGO. With our rapidly-increasing understanding of gravitational waves, primordial black holes have been identified as a likely candidate to explain dark matter.
