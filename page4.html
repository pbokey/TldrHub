On June 24th Britain left the European Union, and the world in shock. The British pound slumped to its lowest point in 30 years as the stock market dropped off. Racism toward minorities and migrants has run rampant as reported harassment and xenophobic actions increased fivefold. In fact, the split itself was fueled by much of the same isolationist rhetoric we’re hearing in the United States. Many of those who voted to leave wanted to “take back full control” of their borders, sealing them to ensure migrants and refugees are unable to come to Britain.

How to Fight Islamic Extremism

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Irfan Kovankaya
  • •
  • in Politics
  • •
  • TLDR

Solutions to win the “war on radical Islam” are often accompanied by vast misunderstandings of the true roots of extremism. We forget we’re fighting more than an extremist group—we’re fighting an ideology. We can defeat ISIS, but we can’t defeat radical Islam. All the collapse of ISIS will do is aide the rise of another extremist group, unless we address the political and socioeconomic grievances of the region that make their citizens so malleable to extremist doctrine.
