Florida’s “Revenge Porn” Law Ensures Risk for Both Victims and Perpetrators

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Jackie Weinell
  • •
  • TLDR

Last October, Florida passed a law criminalizing revenge porn to prevent people from posting sexually explicit images of others online. However, the law is too specific for many offenders to actually be convicted. Some argue this law is a violation of free speech and doesn’t distinguish pornography from images of breastfeeding or nudity in textbooks. Others say it doesn’t suppress free speech because it protects victims from emotional distress, and the law is specific enough to exclude protected materials. Regardless, this controversial law or similar legislation will likely be considered by the Supreme Court.
