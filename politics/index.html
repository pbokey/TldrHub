South Africa: The End of Racial Politics and Single Party Dominance

by Amelia Stern • August 11, 2016

too long; didn't read: The South African political party founded by Nelson Mandela known as the African National Congress has reached its first competitor since the end of Apartheid. The Democratic Alliance has gained increasing control, ensuring that the new political party will have representation in the government. South Africa has not only created plurality in their single dominant party system, but has endorsed a temporary end to the nation’s notorious racial politics. As one of the most corrupt and unequal nations in the world, the future of South Africa not only holds control of its own, but its influence over all of Africa.

Trouble in Rio: The Olympic Burden

  • August 15, 2016
  • •
  • by Andrew Rogan
  • •
  • TLDR

Brazil is struggling with a corrupt national government, weakened economy, brutal police and gang behavior, and the Zika virus, all while trying to host the Olympics. The Olympics Games have grown into a multibillion-dollar event, which has caused revenue, coverage, and scandal to multiply. The IOC pits cities against each other to compete for the Olympic bid, searching for the city can produce the most lavish and extravagant Olympics plan. This increases the burden on host nations, which are often unable to foot the bill. This practice is unsustainable and is proof that the IOC needs to be reformed.
