Florida’s “Revenge Porn” Law Ensures Risk for Both Victims and Perpetrators

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Jaclyn Weinell
  • •
  • TLDR

Last October, Florida passed a law criminalizing the publication of revenge porn. However, the law is too specific for many offenders to actually be convicted. Some argue that this law is a violation of the First Amendment protection of free speech and is so broad that it doesn’t distinguish pornography from images of breastfeeding or nudity in textbooks. Others say it doesn’t suppress free speech because it falls under an established exception, protects people from psychological harm, and is specific enough to exclude protected materials. Regardless, this controversial law or similar legislation will likely be considered by the Supreme Court.

The Coup in Turkey, Explained

  • August 5, 2016
  • •
  • by Zack Goodson
  • •
  • TLDR

In the wake of a failed military coup in Turkey, there’s been little mention by the media of all the major political players involved and the inevitability of the coup based on Turkey’s history. The military was viewed by the founder of Turkey, Ataturk, as a force of intervention in the event the government becomes too corrupt or Islamic. Four military coups have been conducted in the past. Motivations for this recent coup stem from dictatorial autocratic actions made by President Erdogan. Erdogan blames the attack on Fethullah Gulen, an influential imam and an ardent supporter of Turkish democracy.
